# Hair Texture, Density & Health

## Root Cause Analysis

Before adding products, identify the cause:

| Symptom | Likely Cause |
|---------|-------------|
| Dull, brittle | Protein deficiency, heat damage, over-processing |
| Dry, frizzy | Low porosity, moisture barrier damage, humidity |
| Limp, flat | Silicone/product build-up, over-conditioning |
| Excessive shedding | Nutritional deficiency, hormonal, telogen effluvium |
| Slow growth | Scalp health issues, DHT miniaturisation |

## Nutritional Foundations

Hair is made of keratin (protein). Nutritional deficiencies directly affect texture and growth rate.

**Priority bloodwork:**
- **Ferritin:** Target >70 ng/mL. Below 50 is commonly associated with hair texture decline and shedding, even without anaemia.
- **Vitamin D:** Target 50–80 nmol/L. D3 supplementation if deficient.
- **Zinc:** Serum zinc; deficiency causes hair breakage.
- **TSH:** Hypothyroidism causes significant hair quality decline.

**Biotin:** Only effective if you're deficient (rare). Supplementation when replete = no benefit. Don't waste money.

## The Protein-Moisture Balance

Hair needs both structural protein and moisture. Imbalanced hair is either:
- **Protein-overloaded:** Stiff, brittle, snaps cleanly
- **Moisture-overloaded:** Mushy, limp, stretches excessively before breaking

**Bond repair:** Olaplex No.3 (bis-aminopropyl diglycol dimaleate) repairs disulfide bonds broken by chemical processing. 1×/week treatment, leave 10+ min before shampoo.

## Product Chemistry

**Shampoo:**
- Frequency: 2–3× per week (daily strips natural lipids in most hair types)
- Scalp health > hair health. Seborrheic dermatitis suppresses follicle function — ketoconazole 2% shampoo 3×/week if present.
- Avoid SLS in dry/sensitive scalp. Sodium laureth sulfate (SLES) is gentler.

**Conditioner:**
- Always condition mid-lengths to ends. Not scalp (weighs down, can clog follicles).
- Humectants in conditioner: Glycerin, panthenol, aloe vera — attract moisture to hair shaft.

**Leave-in / Treatment:**
- Heat protectant if using hot tools (mandatory above 150°C)
- Argan or jojoba oil on ends only (not roots) — seals cuticle, adds shine

## Scalp Health

The scalp is skin. It requires care:
- **Seborrheic dermatitis (dandruff/flaking):** Antifungal treatment. Zinc pyrithione, selenium sulfide, or ketoconazole shampoo.
- **Scalp massage:** 4 min daily massage increases blood flow to follicles. Some RCT evidence for thickness improvement.
- **Avoid:** Tight hairstyles creating traction (traction alopecia). Heat directly on scalp.

## Physical Handling

- Satin/silk pillowcase: Reduces friction breakage by 40–60% vs cotton. High ROI.
- Wet hair is fragile: Avoid brushing when soaking wet. Detangle with wide-tooth comb starting at ends.
- Heat: Maximum 180°C. Use heat protectant. Limit to 2–3×/week max.

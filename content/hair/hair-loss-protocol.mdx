# Hair Loss / Miniaturization Protocol

## Step 0: Diagnose First

Hair loss has multiple causes. Wrong diagnosis = wrong protocol = money wasted.

**Androgenetic Alopecia (AGA):** Miniaturization pattern — temples, crown. DHT-mediated. Genetic.

**Telogen Effluvium (TE):** Diffuse shedding, often triggered by stress, illness, nutritional deficiency, hormonal change. Usually temporary.

**Nutrient-Deficiency:** Ferritin <50ng/mL is implicated in hair loss even without clinical anemia. Check: ferritin, vitamin D, zinc, thyroid panel.

## Androgenetic Alopecia Stack

### Tier 1 (Evidence: Strong)

| Intervention | Mechanism | Protocol |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Minoxidil 5% topical | Vasodilation, anagen prolongation | 1mL 2×/day to affected areas |
| Finasteride 1mg/day | 5α-reductase inhibitor, blocks DHT | Daily oral — discuss risks with physician |

### Tier 2 (Evidence: Moderate)

| Intervention | Protocol |
|-------------|---------|
| Dermarolling 0.5mm | Weekly, apply minoxidil 24h post-rolling |
| Ketoconazole 2% shampoo | 3×/week, leave 3-5min |
| Low-level laser therapy (LLLT) | Devices with 650-670nm wavelength, 3×/week |

## Telogen Effluvium

- Identify and remove trigger (stress, diet, medication)
- Optimize ferritin to >70ng/mL via supplementation
- TE typically self-resolves in 3-6 months once trigger removed

## Timeline Expectations

- Shedding may *increase* in first 1-2 months of minoxidil (shock loss — old hairs clearing)
- Stabilization: 3-6 months
- Visible regrowth: 6-12 months minimum
- Discontinuation of finasteride = loss of benefit within 12 months

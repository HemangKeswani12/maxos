# Mewing & Craniofacial Optimization

## Mechanism

Mewing refers to maintaining correct tongue posture against the palate, theorized to apply orthopedic force to the maxillary sutures. In adolescents (<25), sutures remain partially unfused — force application can influence growth direction.

## Correct Tongue Posture

1. Full tongue body contact with entire palate
2. Tip behind upper incisors (NOT touching them)
3. Back third of tongue elevated — this is the hard part
4. Lips sealed, teeth lightly touching or 1-2mm apart
5. Nasal breathing exclusively

## Adjunct Protocols

### Masseter Hypertrophy
- Mastic gum: 20-30min/day
- Produces measurable jaw angle definition over 6-12 months
- Natural gum, xylitol — NOT sugar

### Nasal Breathing
- Mouth tape during sleep if habitual mouth breathing
- Nasal rinse (saline) if congested — unblocks passages

## Evidence & Limitations

**Established:** Correct tongue posture is associated with better craniofacial development in children (orthodontic literature)

**Disputed:** Active remodeling via tongue posture in adults (>25). Sutures largely fused. Soft tissue and muscle changes more plausible than skeletal.

**Timeline:**
- Soft tissue/posture changes: 3-6 months
- Possible skeletal changes (adolescents): 12-24 months
- Adults: Primarily aesthetic improvement from masseter development

## Common Errors

- Tongue tip too forward (touching teeth) — incorrect
- Partial tongue contact — ineffective
- Swallowing with tongue off palate — counterproductive

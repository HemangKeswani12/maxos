# Redness, Rosacea & Reactive Skin

## Diagnosis

Rosacea subtypes require different approaches:

| Subtype | Presentation | Primary Treatment |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| ETR (Erythematotelangiectatic) | Persistent redness, flushing, visible vessels | Laser/IPL, topical brimonidine |
| Papulopustular | Inflammatory bumps mimicking acne, redness | Topical ivermectin, azelaic acid |
| Phymatous | Skin thickening, especially nose | Isotretinoin (severe), laser |
| Ocular | Eye involvement | Ophthalmologist |

## Trigger Elimination First

Document and identify your triggers. Common culprits:

- **UV exposure** (most universal trigger — SPF is mandatory)
- Alcohol (vasodilation, prolonged flushing)
- Spicy foods / capsaicin
- Extreme temperatures (hot drinks, hot showers, cold wind)
- Exercise (temporary — unavoidable; allow for post-exercise cooldown)
- Certain skincare ingredients: Witch hazel, alcohol, menthol, high-% acids, fragrance

## Evidence-Based Topicals

### Tier 1
- **Azelaic acid 15–20%:** Anti-inflammatory, tyrosinase inhibitor, reduces redness and papules. Prescription strength (20%) significantly more effective.
- **Topical ivermectin 1% cream (Rx):** Addresses Demodex mite overgrowth component. Best evidence for papulopustular subtype.

### Tier 2
- **Metronidazole 0.75–1% (Rx):** Long-standing treatment, anti-inflammatory, reduces papules.
- **Brimonidine 0.33% gel (Rx):** Alpha-adrenergic agonist. Rapidly constricts blood vessels — significant redness reduction within 30 min. Does not treat underlying condition.

### OTC Support
- **Niacinamide 5%:** Anti-inflammatory, barrier repair, well-tolerated. Start here.
- **Centella asiatica/cica:** Calming, barrier repair. Excellent in sensitive formulas.

## The Protocol

**AM:**
1. Gentle cream cleanser (no foam/SLS)
2. Niacinamide 5% serum
3. Moisturiser with ceramides + centella
4. Mineral SPF 50+ (zinc oxide-based — no chemical filters)

**PM:**
1. Gentle cleanser
2. Azelaic acid 15% OR prescribed topical
3. Ceramide-rich moisturiser

**Avoid in all formulations:** Fragrance, alcohol, witch hazel, menthol, eucalyptus, peppermint, essential oils.

## What Doesn't Apply

If skin is reactive but NOT rosacea (contact dermatitis, eczema, seborrheic dermatitis), the protocol differs. Patch test all new products. Introduce one product per 2-week window.

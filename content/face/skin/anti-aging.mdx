# Anti-Aging & Collagen Preservation

## Evidence Hierarchy

**Tier 1 (Strong RCT evidence):**
- SPF 50+ daily (prevents ~80% of visible photoaging)
- Tretinoin 0.05–0.1% (only topical proven to remodel dermis)
- Smoking cessation (destroys collagen, reduces microcirculation)

**Tier 2 (Good evidence):**
- Vitamin C 15–20% (antioxidant, collagen cofactor)
- AHA regular exfoliation (cell turnover acceleration)
- Niacinamide 5–10% (barrier, hyperpigmentation, tone)

**Tier 3 (Theoretical/early evidence):**
- Peptides (palmitoyl pentapeptide, Matrixyl)
- Growth factors
- Bakuchiol (retinol alternative, gentler, less evidence)

## The Daily Protocol

### Morning
1. Gentle cleanser
2. Vitamin C serum (L-ascorbic acid 15–20%)
3. Peptide serum (optional — apply on damp skin)
4. Moisturiser
5. **SPF 50+**

### Evening
1. Cleanser (double cleanse if wearing SPF)
2. **Tretinoin 0.025–0.1%** (apply to fully dry skin — wet skin increases irritation)
3. Wait 20–30 min
4. Ceramide moisturiser

### Weekly
- AHA exfoliation (glycolic or lactic acid 10%) on a non-retinoid night

## Mechanism: Why Tretinoin Works

Tretinoin (all-trans retinoic acid) binds nuclear retinoic acid receptors (RARs), which:
1. Upregulate collagen synthesis in the dermis
2. Inhibit MMP enzymes that degrade existing collagen
3. Accelerate epidermal cell turnover
4. Increase hyaluronic acid production

No other topical has this mechanism with this level of evidence.

## Starting Tretinoin

- Begin at 0.025%, 2–3×/week
- Titrate to 5× per week over 8 weeks
- After 3–4 months, step up to 0.05%
- Irritation ("retinoid dermatitis") in weeks 1–4 is expected. Reduce frequency, do not stop.
- Buffering: Apply moisturiser first, then tretinoin, then another moisturiser layer

## Lifestyle Factors

| Factor | Mechanism | Impact |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Smoking | Nicotine constricts blood vessels, carbon monoxide reduces O2 delivery, free radical damage to collagen | Major |
| UV exposure | MMP activation, oxidative stress, DNA damage | Major |
| Sleep deprivation | Cortisol elevation degrades collagen, reduces GH-driven repair | Significant |
| High-sugar diet | Glycation cross-links collagen fibres, reduces elasticity | Moderate |
| Alcohol | Dehydration, vitamin A antagonist (competes with retinol metabolism) | Moderate |

# Hypertrophy Fundamentals

## The Drivers

Five variables determine hypertrophy, ranked by evidence:

1. **Mechanical tension** (load × time under tension)
2. **Proximity to failure** (training hard enough)
3. **Volume** (sufficient sets per muscle)
4. **Progressive overload** (increasing stimulus over time)
5. **Protein availability**

Everything else (pump, soreness, variety, supplements except creatine and caffeine) is secondary.

## Training Variables

### Volume
- 10–20 working sets per muscle per week
- Beginners: Start at 10. Add 2 sets per mesocycle.
- More is not always better — recovery is the rate limiter

### Intensity
- 6–30 reps is the effective range
- **All rep ranges build muscle** if taken to within 2 reps of failure
- For compound movements: 6–10 reps (allows heavier loading)
- For isolation: 12–20 reps (reduces joint stress)

### Proximity to Failure
- Most hypertrophy occurs in the final 3–5 reps of a set
- RIR 2–3 (stop 2–3 reps before failure) for compounds
- RIR 0–1 for isolation movements
- Never go to absolute failure on compound movements — risk:reward is poor

### Frequency
- 2× per muscle per week is optimal for most
- Distributes weekly volume and keeps protein synthesis elevated more consistently

### Progressive Overload
- Add load, reps, or sets over time
- Track every workout. If you aren't progressing, you aren't growing.

## Nutrition

| Variable | Target |
|---------|--------|
| Protein | 1.6–2.2g/kg bodyweight |
| Caloric surplus | +200–400 kcal/day for lean bulk |
| Carbohydrates | Primary fuel for training, not essential but performance-relevant |
| Meal timing | Protein within 2h before and after training is slightly beneficial |

**Protein quality:** Complete amino acid profiles from meat, eggs, dairy, or soy. Plant proteins require higher total intake.

## Recovery

- **Sleep:** 7–9 hours. GH peaks in early sleep cycles. Sleep deprivation reduces protein synthesis by up to 30%.
- **Deload:** Every 6–8 weeks, reduce volume by 40–50% for 1 week. Allows structural recovery.
- **Soreness:** Not a measure of effectiveness. Decreases with training consistency — this is adaptation, not a problem.

## Supplements (Evidence-Based Only)

| Supplement | Benefit | Dosage |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Creatine monohydrate | +5–15% strength, increased water in muscle | 5g/day, any time |
| Caffeine | Performance, strength, endurance | 3–6mg/kg, pre-workout |
| Protein powder | Convenient protein source — no unique anabolic effect | As needed to hit targets |

## Common Mistakes

- **Not tracking workouts:** Cannot progress what you don't measure
- **Too much variety:** Novelty feels productive. Consistency builds muscle.
- **Insufficient protein:** Most common nutritional deficiency
- **Too much cardio during bulk:** Interferes with strength adaptations if concurrent
- **Not sleeping enough:** The most underrated performance variable

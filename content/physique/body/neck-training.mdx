# Neck Development Protocol

## Why It Matters

Neck circumference has a substantial impact on perceived overall size and presence. It frames the jaw and head, contributes to shoulder-to-head ratio, and is one of the most visible muscles in daily life.

Neck musculature is severely undertrained in most programs because:
1. It's not emphasised in standard routines
2. People are unsure how to train it directly
3. Fear of injury

**Primary muscles:**
- Sternocleidomastoid (SCM) — flexion, rotation
- Upper trapezius — elevation, extension support
- Splenius capitis/cervicis — extension, lateral flexion
- Suboccipitals — fine extension and rotation

## Safety Protocol

**Do not rush neck training.** The cervical spine is load-sensitive.

- Week 1–2: Bodyweight only (hand resistance)
- Week 3–4: Light resistance band
- Week 5+: Introduce external load (plate, cable)
- Start with the lowest weight possible for all new exercises
- Zero pain at any point — stop immediately if sharp or shooting pain occurs

## Exercise Protocol

### Neck Flexion (Targets SCM and anterior)
- Setup: Lie on bench, head hanging off the edge
- Movement: Controlled nodding — chin to chest and back
- Load: Plate held on forehead with hands (pad with towel)
- Sets/Reps: 3×15–20
- Start bodyweight only

### Neck Extension (Targets posterior neck and splenius)
- Setup: Lie face-down on bench, head hanging off the edge
- Movement: Head extension — look up toward ceiling
- Load: Plate on back of head
- Sets/Reps: 3×15–20

### Lateral Neck Flexion (Targets SCM, scalenes)
- Setup: Side-lying or seated
- Movement: Ear toward shoulder, controlled
- Load: Hand resistance or light plate
- Sets/Reps: 3×12–15 each side

### Isometric Neck Work
- Four-direction isometric holds (press hand against head in each direction)
- Hold 5–10s each. 3 rounds per direction.
- Excellent for beginners and warm-up.

## Trapezius (Frames the Neck)

The upper trapezius creates the visual slope from neck to shoulder — critical for the "thick" appearance.

- **Barbell shrugs:** 4×15–20. Full elevation, brief pause at top, controlled descent
- **Dumbbell shrugs:** Better ROM for some
- **Overhead carry:** Farmer's carry overhead variation — extreme trap activation

## Frequency

Neck muscles recover quickly. 2–4×/week is appropriate.

## Measuring Progress

Measure neck circumference at the same point each time (at the level of the larynx/Adam's apple). 
Expected gain: 1–2.5cm in 12–20 weeks of consistent direct training.

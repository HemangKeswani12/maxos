# Sleep Optimization for Physical Improvement

## Why Sleep Is Not Optional

During sleep, particularly slow-wave (deep) sleep:

- **Growth hormone (GH) peaks** — primary anabolic signal. 70% of daily GH is released in the first 2 hours of sleep.
- **Protein synthesis increases** — tissue repair and muscle building occurs
- **Cortisol drops** — cortisol is catabolic (breaks down muscle) and promotes fat storage
- **Skin repair peaks** — epithelial cell division at highest rate
- **Testosterone production** — 70% of daily testosterone produced during sleep in males
- **Memory consolidation** — movement patterns and skills encoded

**A single night of <6h sleep:**
- Reduces testosterone by 10–15%
- Decreases protein synthesis by up to 30%
- Increases cortisol significantly
- Reduces insulin sensitivity (promotes fat storage)
- Worsens skin barrier function

## Target: 7–9 Hours in Bed

Not just hours in bed — quality matters. Sleep architecture:
- **N1/N2 (light sleep):** Transition stages
- **N3 (slow-wave/deep):** GH, physical repair, immune function
- **REM:** Memory, emotional regulation

You cycle through these ~4–6 times per night. Disrupted cycles = less time in N3 and REM.

## Protocol

### Pre-Sleep (90 min before bed)
- **No blue light:** Screens suppress melatonin synthesis by up to 50%. Use blue-light filter or avoid screens.
- **Temperature:** Begin cooling the room. Core body temperature must drop 1–2°C to initiate sleep.
- **No food:** 90+ min before bed. Digestion disrupts sleep architecture.
- **No alcohol:** Sedation is not sleep. Alcohol fragments sleep architecture, dramatically reduces REM.

### Sleep Environment
- **Temperature: 17–19°C.** The most impactful single environmental variable.
- **Darkness:** Blackout curtains or sleep mask. Any light suppresses melatonin.
- **Silence or white noise:** Eliminates arousal from intermittent sounds.

### Morning
- **Light exposure within 30 min of waking:** Anchors circadian rhythm. 10 min outside, or SAD lamp in winter.
- **Consistent wake time 7 days/week:** The most powerful circadian rhythm regulator.

## Supplements (Evidence-Based)

| Supplement | Mechanism | Dosage | Evidence |
|-----------|-----------|--------|---------|
| Magnesium glycinate | GABA-A modulation, muscle relaxation | 200–400mg | Moderate |
| L-theanine | GABA/glycine, reduces sleep latency | 100–200mg | Moderate |
| Melatonin | Phase-shifts circadian clock | 0.5–1mg (low dose) | Strong for shift/jet lag |
| Ashwagandha | Cortisol reduction | 300–600mg KSM-66 | Moderate |

**Avoid high-dose melatonin (5–10mg):** Does not improve sleep quality. May desensitise receptors. Use 0.5–1mg.

## Training and Sleep

- Intense exercise within 3h of bed can delay sleep onset (sympathetic activation)
- Finish heavy sessions by 6pm ideally
- Morning training + evening light cardio is optimal for sleep quality

# Body Fat Reduction Protocol

## The Physics

Fat loss requires sustained caloric deficit. No food combination, timing strategy, or supplement overrides this.

**TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)** = BMR × Activity Multiplier

Use an online calculator for baseline, then validate by tracking intake vs. weight changes for 2 weeks.

## Deficit Targets

| Rate | Deficit | Body Composition |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| Aggressive | 700–1000 kcal/day | Faster fat loss, more muscle loss risk |
| Moderate | 300–500 kcal/day | Optimal for muscle preservation |
| Conservative | 150–250 kcal/day | Very slow, good for advanced athletes |

**Recommendation:** Moderate deficit for most people. Faster rates require meticulous protein intake and heavy training.

## Protein: The Critical Variable

Protein is the single most impactful dietary variable during a cut.

- **Target:** 2.0–2.4g/kg bodyweight per day
- **Why:** Preserves lean mass, has high thermic effect (25–30% of calories burned digesting it), high satiety per calorie
- **Priority:** Hit protein target before worrying about carb/fat distribution

## Resistance Training During Fat Loss

**Do not stop training during a cut.** The mechanical tension from resistance training is the signal to preserve muscle.

- Maintain training intensity (same loads as during bulk, or close to it)
- Volume can reduce slightly (10–20%) if recovery is compromised
- Cardio is additive — not the primary driver of fat loss

## Cardio

| Type | Protocol | Benefit |
|------|---------|---------|
| LISS | 30–45 min walks/cycling, 4–5×/week | Low fatigue, creates steady deficit |
| HIIT | 20 min, 3×/week | Time-efficient, EPOC effect |
| NEAT | Increase daily steps to 8,000–10,000 | Most underrated fat loss tool |

NEAT (non-exercise activity thermogenesis) — fidgeting, walking, standing — accounts for up to 500 kcal/day variance between individuals. Get steps up.

## Tracking

- Weigh daily, use 7-day rolling average (eliminates water/food weight noise)
- Track food for at least 2 weeks — most people systematically underestimate intake
- Progress photos every 4 weeks in consistent lighting/time/clothing

## Diet Breaks & Adaptive Thermogenesis

After 8–10 weeks of deficit, metabolism adapts downward. TDEE decreases.

**Diet break:** Return to maintenance calories for 1–2 weeks. This partially reverses adaptive thermogenesis and psychologically resets compliance.

## Troubleshooting Stalled Fat Loss

1. **Audit tracking accuracy** — are condiments, oils, and sauces being tracked?
2. **Reduce deficit by 100 kcal** — may have overestimated TDEE
3. **Increase NEAT** — add 2,000 steps/day
4. **Increase protein** — ensures muscle retention
5. **Check sleep** — poor sleep increases ghrelin (hunger), decreases leptin (satiety), cortisol promotes fat storage

# Anterior Pelvic Tilt Correction

## What It Is

Anterior pelvic tilt (APT) occurs when the front of the pelvis drops downward and the back rises — increasing lumbar lordosis. It creates:
- Apparent gut even at low body fat
- Lower back pain and compression
- Hamstring tightness
- Weak, underactivated glutes
- Reduced athletic performance

## Assessment

Lie on your back on a flat floor. In ideal alignment, your lower back should be about 1–2cm above the floor. If you can slide your whole hand under, APT is likely. If your back is completely flat or pressed to the floor, posterior pelvic tilt is possible.

Standing: Look from the side — pelvis should be roughly level. APT = "duck butt" / pronounced arch.

## Root Causes

| Tight/Short | Weak/Inhibited |
|-------------|---------------|
| Hip flexors (psoas, iliacus) | Glutes (max and med) |
| Rectus femoris (quad) | Hamstrings |
| Lumbar extensors (erector spinae) | Deep core (transverse abdominis) |
| TFL | Abdominals |

## Correction Protocol

### Daily Stretching (10–15 min)

**Hip flexor stretch (kneeling lunge):** Front foot forward, back knee down. Drive hip forward while maintaining upright torso. Hold 60s each side. You must feel it at the FRONT of the hip, not the knee.

**Rectus femoris stretch:** Stand, grab ankle behind you, posterior tilt pelvis actively. Do not let lumbar extend. Hold 45s each side.

**Lumbar rotation stretch:** Lie on back, knees bent, let knees fall side to side. 10 reps each side.

### 3×/Week Strengthening

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Target |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Hip thrusts/glute bridge | 3×15–20 | Glute max |
| Romanian deadlift | 3×10–12 | Hamstrings + glutes |
| Dead bug | 3×10 | Deep core |
| Hollow body hold | 3×20–30s | Anterior core |
| Single-leg glute bridge | 3×12 each | Glute med, hip stability |
| Posterior pelvic tilt (standing) | Throughout day | Habit repatterning |

### Daily Cue

While standing: actively squeeze glutes and tuck pelvis. Not an exaggerated posterior tilt, but neutral. Repeat every hour throughout the day for 10 seconds.

## Timeline

- **4 weeks:** Hip flexor flexibility measurably improved. Cue starts to become habit.
- **8 weeks:** Resting pelvic position visibly improved.
- **12–16 weeks:** Near-neutral pelvis at rest without conscious effort.

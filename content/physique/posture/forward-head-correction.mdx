# Forward Head Posture Correction

## Mechanism

FHP results from deep cervical flexor (DCF) inhibition combined with upper cervical extensor tightness. Every 2.5cm of forward head position adds ~10kg of effective load on the cervical spine.

## Assessment

Stand naturally beside a wall. Ideal: ear canal, shoulder, hip, knee, ankle in vertical alignment. FHP = ear anterior to shoulder.

## Corrective Protocol

### Daily (5-10 min)

**Chin Tucks (3×15)**
- Stand or sit upright
- Pull chin straight back — NOT down
- Hold 5 seconds at end range
- Feel: stretch at suboccipital region, engagement at deep neck flexors

**Wall Angels (3×10)**
- Back flat against wall, arms in "W" position
- Slide arms up to "Y" — maintain wall contact throughout
- Corrects thoracic kyphosis contributing to FHP

### 3x/Week (Gym)

| Exercise | Sets×Reps | Target |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Face pulls | 3×15-20 | Rear delts, external rotators |
| Band pull-aparts | 3×20 | Rhomboids, mid traps |
| Lat pulldowns | 3×10-12 | Lower traps, lats |
| Prone Y-T-W raises | 3×10 each | Lower/mid traps |

### Daily Stretches

- Levator scapulae: 30s × 3 each side
- Upper trapezius: 30s × 3 each side
- Pec minor: doorframe stretch 30s × 3

## Ergonomics

- Monitor top: eye level at top third of screen
- Chair: hips ≥ 90°, lumbar supported
- Phone: raise to eye level (text neck = compounded FHP)

## Timeline

Consistent 8-week protocol yields measurable improvement in head position alignment.

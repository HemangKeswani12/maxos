# Thoracic Kyphosis Correction

## What It Is

Thoracic kyphosis is excessive rounding of the upper-to-mid back (T1–T12). It is distinguished from normal thoracic curvature (physiological kyphosis exists) by degree of curve and functional impairment.

**Functional effects:**
- Forward head posture (head follows thoracic curve forward)
- Shoulder impingement risk (rotator cuff and subacromial space)
- Reduced height perception (slouching compresses apparent height)
- Reduced overhead ROM
- Cosmetic: rounded appearance, "hunchback" silhouette

## Causes

- Prolonged sitting without lumbar support
- Excessive chest pressing without balancing pulling work
- Tight pecs and anterior shoulder capsule
- Weak thoracic extensors and lower trapezius

## Mobility Protocol (Daily, 10–15 min)

### Thoracic Extension Over Foam Roller
Position roller under thoracic spine. Support head with hands. Allow gravity to extend over the roller 30–60 seconds per position (upper, mid, lower thoracic). Works on thoracic extension ROM.

### Wall Slides
- Back flat against wall (occiput, thoracic spine, sacrum all touching)
- Arms in "W" position, elbows at 90°
- Slide arms up the wall to "Y" while maintaining all contact points
- 3×10–12. This is the gold standard thoracic mobility drill.

### Cat-Cow
3×10, maximising both flexion and extension. The extension phase is the target.

### Thoracic Rotation (90/90)
Lie on side, knees at 90°. Stack knees, arms out in front. Rotate top arm to ceiling and behind. 3×10 each side.

### Quadruped Thoracic Extension
On hands and knees, thread one arm under the body. 3×10 each side.

## Strengthening Protocol (3×/week)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Target |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Prone Y raises | 3×15 | Lower trapezius |
| Prone T raises | 3×15 | Mid trapezius |
| Face pulls | 3×15–20 | Rear delts, upper back |
| Seated cable rows (elbows wide) | 3×12 | Rhomboids, mid traps |
| Band pull-aparts | 3×20 | Posterior shoulder capsule |
| Reverse flyes | 3×15 | Posterior deltoid, rhomboids |

## Programming Integration

**Pull:Push ratio should be 2:1 minimum.** Most programs are push-heavy. Bench press → row. Dumbbell press → face pull. OHP → band pull-apart.

Excessive pec development without rowing creates anterior shoulder impingement and worsens thoracic kyphosis.

## Timeline

- **2 weeks:** Improved thoracic extension ROM, reduced "locked" feeling
- **6 weeks:** Resting posture measurably improved
- **12–20 weeks:** Near-normal thoracic curve at rest without active effort
